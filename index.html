Number Operations & Place Value

Understanding Place Value

Example: In the number 12,345:

  • The 1 is worth 10,000 (ten thousands place)
  • The 2 is worth 2,000 (thousands place)
  • The 3 is worth 300 (hundreds place)
  • The 4 is worth 40 (tens place)
  • The 5 is worth 5 (units place)

💡 Quick Tip

When finding the value of a digit, look at its position and multiply by the place value!

Number Properties

Cube Numbers: Numbers that result from multiplying a number by itself three times.

Examples: 1³ = 1, 2³ = 8, 3³ = 27, 4³ = 64, 5³ = 125

Fractions & Decimals

Comparing Fractions

To compare fractions, convert them to the same denominator or decimal form.

Example: Which is larger: ½ or ⅜?

Convert to same denominator: ½ = 4/8
Compare: 4/8 vs 3/8
Answer: ½ is larger than ⅜

Interactive Question

B) ¾
C) ⅝

E) ⅚

Converting Fractions to Decimals

Divide the numerator by the denominator:

  • ¼ = 1 ÷ 4 = 0.25
  • ⅓ = 1 ÷ 3 = 0.333...
  • ¾ = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

Percentages & Proportions

Calculating Percentages

Example: What is 20% of 150?

Convert percentage to decimal: 20% = 0.20
Multiply: 0.20 × 150 = 30
Answer: 20% of 150 is 30
Percentage Formula:
Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Ratios and Proportions

Example: If the ratio of boys to girls is 3:2 and there are 15 boys, how many girls are there?

Find the multiplier: 15 ÷ 3 = 5
Apply to girls: 2 × 5 = 10
Answer: There are 10 girls

Bar Charts and Tables

When reading bar charts:

  • Check the scale on the y-axis
  • Read values carefully at the top of each bar
  • Look for patterns and trends

💡 Quick Tip

For averages: Add all values and divide by the number of values. Mode = most frequent value, Median = middle value when ordered.

Geometry & Shapes

Properties of 2D Shapes

Polygons:

  • Triangle: 3 sides, 3 angles (sum = 180°)
  • Quadrilateral: 4 sides, 4 angles (sum = 360°)
  • Pentagon: 5 sides, 5 angles
  • Hexagon: 6 sides, 6 angles

3D Shapes

Common 3D shapes and their properties:

  • Cube: 6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices
  • Cuboid: 6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices
  • Cylinder: 3 faces (2 circular, 1 curved), 2 edges
  • Sphere: 1 curved face, no edges, no vertices

💡 Angle Rules

Remember: Angles in a triangle = 180°, Angles in a quadrilateral = 360°, Angles on a straight line = 180°

Area, Perimeter & Volume

Key Formulas:
Rectangle Area = length × width
Triangle Area = ½ × base × height
Circle Area = π × radius²
Perimeter = sum of all sides

Calculating Area of Composite Shapes

Example: Find the area of an L-shaped room:

Break into rectangles: 6m × 4m and 3m × 2m
Calculate each area: 24m² and 6m²
Add together: 24 + 6 = 30m²

Coordinates & Graphs

Understanding Coordinates

Coordinates are written as (x, y):

  • x-coordinate: how far along (horizontal)
  • y-coordinate: how far up (vertical)
  • Example: Point (3, 5) is 3 units right, 5 units up

Algebra & Patterns

Number Sequences

Finding the pattern:

Sequence: 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, ...

Find the difference: +4 each time
Next term: 19 + 4 = 23
Rule: Start at 3, add 4 each time

Function Machines

Example: Input → ×3 → +2 → Output

If input is 5: 5 × 3 = 15, then 15 + 2 = 17

If output is 20: 20 - 2 = 18, then 18 ÷ 3 = 6

Time & Measurement

Time Calculations

Adding and subtracting times:

Example: 2 hours 45 minutes + 1 hour 30 minutes

Add hours: 2 + 1 = 3 hours
Add minutes: 45 + 30 = 75 minutes
Convert: 75 minutes = 1 hour 15 minutes
Total: 3 + 1 hour 15 minutes = 4 hours 15 minutes
